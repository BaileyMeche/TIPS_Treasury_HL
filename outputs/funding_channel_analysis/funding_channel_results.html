Interpretation

Longer-tenor mispricing half-lives exhibit larger positive loadings on the synthetic CIP bases and money-market wedges relative to short-tenor spreads, suggesting that balance-sheet stress propagates more forcefully into long-maturity trades. Nonetheless, R-squared values remain modest and correlation magnitudes are below 0.4, consistent with partial market segmentation wherein specialist desks intermediate distinct tenors.

The Funding Stress principal component loads positively on both CIP bases and the RRP-bill wedge while loading negatively on the swap-spread proxy. This configuration mirrors a scenario in which funding constraints widen basis spreads and compress swap spreads; regression sensitivities imply slower convergence when the composite stress index rises, especially for 10Y and 20Y tenors.
